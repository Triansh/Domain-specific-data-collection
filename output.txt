('**Aenigmatite** (IMA symbol: Aen), also known as _Cossyrite_ after Cossyra,\n'
 'the ancient name of Pantelleria, is a sodium, iron, titanium inosilicate\n'
 'mineral. The chemical formula is Na2Fe2+5TiSi6O20 and its structure '
 'consists\n'
 'of single tetrahedral chains with a repeat unit of four and complex side\n'
 'branches. It forms brown to black triclinic lamellar crystals. It has Mohs\n'
 'hardness of 5.5 to 6 and specific gravity of 3.74 to 3.85. Aenigmatite forms '
 'a\n'
 'solid-solution series with wilkinsonite, Na2Fe2+4Fe3+2Si6O20.\n'
 '\n'
 'Aenigmatite is primarily found in peralkaline volcanic rocks, pegmatites, '
 'and\n'
 'granites as well as silica-poor intrusive rocks. It was first described by\n'
 'August Breithaupt in 1865 for an occurrence in the Ilimaussaq intrusive\n'
 'complex of southwest Greenland. Its name comes from **αίνιγμα** , the Greek\n'
 'word for "riddle".\n'
 '\n'
 'It was also reported from the Kaidun meteorite, possibly a Mars meteorite,\n'
 'which landed in March 1980 in South Yemen. Other notable studied '
 'occurrences\n'
 'include:\n'
 '\n'
 '  * Narsaarsuk and elsewhere in Greenland.\n'
 '  * The Khibiny and Lovozero alkaline massifs on Kola Peninsula, Russia.\n'
 '  * The Yenisei Range, Krasnoyarsk Krai, Russia.\n'
 '  * The volcanic island of Pantelleria, Italy.\n'
 '  * In the US, from Granite Mountain, near Little Rock, Pulaski County, '
 'Arkansas, and Santa Rosa, Sonoma County, California.\n'
 '  * In Australia, from Warrumbungle volcano, Nandewar volcano, and the Mount '
 'Warning complex, New South Wales; and the Peak Range Province, Queensland.\n'
 '  * In Canada, from Mount Edziza, the Ilgachuz and Rainbow Range shield '
 'complexes.\n'
 '  * From Logan Point quarry, Dunedin volcano, New Zealand.')
